About the programme

Omics technologies are increasingly used in diagnostics and biomedical research. This programme builds practical competency in data analysis and interpretation across key omics pillars.

Target audience

  • Postgraduate students
  • Laboratory scientists
  • Researchers and clinicians
  • Healthcare professionals interested in omics

Learning outcomes

  • Understand complete analysis workflows
  • Perform core QC and interpretation steps
  • Produce reproducible outputs and figures

Programme format

  • In-person, guided practical sessions
  • Curated datasets and step-by-step exercises
  • Certificate of participation

Three-day programme

The training is delivered as a structured three-day programme, combining lectures, hands-on sessions, and interactive discussions.

Day Focus Key activities
Day 1
1 April		 Opening & Genomics
  • Programme opening and orientation
  • Introduction to NGS data types and workflows
  • Quality control and preprocessing
  • Alignment and variant calling concepts
Day 2
2 April		 Transcriptomics & Proteomics
  • RNA-seq experimental design and QC
  • Differential expression analysis
  • Proteomics data types and preprocessing
  • Differential abundance and interpretation
Day 3
3 April		 Multi-omics Integration & Closing
  • Principles of multi-omics integration
  • Integrated analysis and visualization
  • Case studies and discussion
  • Closing session and certificates
