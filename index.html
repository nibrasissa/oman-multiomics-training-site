About the programme

This programme provides a guided introduction to practical multi-omics analysis workflows, including key concepts, quality control, interpretation strategies, and integration approaches. Sessions combine lectures, demonstrations, and applied exercises.

Who should attend

  • Students interested in omics analysis
  • Researchers working with sequencing / omics data
  • Healthcare professionals seeking applied understanding

Learning outcomes

  • Understand core genomics, transcriptomics, and proteomics workflows
  • Interpret results and common QC metrics
  • Apply integration principles across modalities

Format

  • Structured 3-day programme (1–3 April)
  • Interactive sessions + case discussions
  • Closing and certificates

Three-day programme

The programme is organized by day and focus area. A detailed agenda (timings) may be shared upon confirmation.

Day Focus Key activities
Day 1
1 April		 Opening & Genomics
  • Programme opening and orientation
  • Introduction to NGS data types and workflows
  • Quality control and preprocessing
  • Alignment and variant analysis concepts
Day 2
2 April		 Transcriptomics & Proteomics
  • RNA-seq design principles and QC
  • Differential expression analysis and interpretation
  • Proteomics data types and preprocessing overview
  • Differential abundance and biological interpretation
Day 3
3 April		 Multi-omics Integration & Closing
  • Principles of multi-omics integration
  • Integrated analysis and visualization approaches
  • Case studies and discussion
  • Closing session and certificates
