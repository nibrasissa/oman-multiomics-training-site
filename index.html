About the training

This advanced training programme is designed for students, researchers, and healthcare professionals seeking a structured understanding of modern omics technologies and their integration in biomedical research and clinical applications. The programme combines conceptual foundations with applied workflows, focusing on interpretation and best practices rather than heavy coding.

Who should attend

  • Students in biomedical and life sciences
  • Researchers working with omics data
  • Clinical and laboratory professionals

Learning outcomes

  • Recognize key data types and workflow stages
  • Interpret QC metrics and analytical outputs
  • Apply integration logic across omics layers

Format

  • Three-day structured programme (1–3 April)
  • Applied examples and case discussions
  • Closing session and certificates

Programme

The programme is organized by day and focus area. A detailed agenda (timings) will be shared with registered participants.

Day Focus Key content
Day 1
1 April		 Opening & Genomics
  • Opening session and orientation
  • Introduction to NGS technologies and genomics workflows
  • Quality control and preprocessing concepts
  • Variant analysis concepts and interpretation strategies
Day 2
2 April		 Transcriptomics & Proteomics
  • RNA-seq design and interpretation
  • Differential expression: concepts and reading outputs
  • Proteomics data structure and interpretation overview
  • Differential protein analysis and biological context
Day 3
3 April		 Multi-Omics Integration & Closing
  • Integration principles and common strategies
  • Pathway-level interpretation and integrated reporting
  • Case studies and discussion
  • Closing session and certificates
